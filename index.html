  • Ransomware: Should organizations pay or not?

    Ransomware

    Ransomware is malicious software designed to limit a user's access to their system by encrypting a user's files or locking them out of the user's system until the user pays the ransom. Paying the ransom depends on the organization's situation and whether they are willing to take the risk. The reasons to not pay the ransom: further encouragement of attacks, milked for multiple payments, legal issues, and the attacker might not return the data. Encouragement of attacks, not only are the attackers better funded if they want to attack another organization, but the organization also looks like a good target for other attackers. Milked for multiple payments, sometimes after organizations pay the ransom, the attackers want more. The attackers might not even return all the data. After all of the other reasons, there are legal issues; depending on where the organization resides, paying that ransom can be seen as 'funding terrorism' if the attackers are from nation-states that have problems with the country the victim organization resides or seen as enabling criminals to commit more attacks. The reasons to pay the ransom would be an easier recovery and to protect the users. If the data is sensitive, the data recovery takes a while, or it is more expensive to recover the data, then the organization should consider it. Again this all depends on the organization's situation. Inspired by Tech Target
